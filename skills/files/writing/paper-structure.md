# Paper Structure for Empirical Research

## Standard structure for IS/Econ/Finance papers

### 1. Introduction (1.5–2 pages)
- Hook: motivating fact or phenomenon
- Research question: one clear sentence
- Identification strategy: one paragraph
- Main findings: 3-4 bullet points with specific magnitudes
- Contribution: what this adds to the literature (3 items max)
- Road map: two sentences

### 2. Related Literature (1–2 pages)
- Organize by research stream, not chronologically
- End each paragraph with how your paper differs
- Do NOT survey — synthesize. Show where the gaps are.

### 3. Institutional Context / Background (0.5–1 page)
- Describe the setting that motivates the research question
- Explain the mechanism that could drive results
- Non-expert reader should understand why this setting is informative

### 4. Data (1–1.5 pages)
- Source, time period, coverage
- Sample selection and exclusions (with counts)
- Variable construction
- Summary statistics table

### 5. Empirical Strategy (1–1.5 pages)
- Econometric specification with equation
- Identification argument
- Threats and mitigating factors

### 6. Results (2–3 pages)
- Main table: baseline specification
- Robustness section (at minimum: alternative specifications, subsamples)
- Mechanism test (what drives the result)

### 7. Discussion / Conclusion (0.5–1 page)
- Restate findings without repeating the results section
- Implications for practice / policy
- Limitations
- Future research (one specific question only)

## LaTeX conventions
- Tables: use `booktabs` (no vertical lines)
- Figures: caption below, label with `fig:name`
- Equations: number all displayed equations
- Standard errors in parentheses, significance stars in notes
